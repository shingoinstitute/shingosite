Lean-Driven Innovation

Billy Taylor & Norbert Majerus

Lean has revolutionized manufacturing processes around the world. Unfortunately, that has not been the case for processes dealing with business, administration or R&D. That is very unfortunate because the benefits of applying lean to a process like R&D or innovation creation can be even larger than the benefits achieved in manufacturing. The key is to know how to apply lean in an R&D environment. Goodyear applied lean to what R&D can do for the business, not to how lean can save money in R&D. This helped Goodyear create more and better value for the customer and more profit for the company. Other key factors in the transformation were developing and continuously improving a lean process, collaborating across the value chain, managing the cultural change that lean demands, creating the correct organization to leverage lean, and training and correctly managing people. In this presentation, Billy Taylor and Norbert Majerus we will describe the Goodyear journey, the lean principles that were applied, and the achieved results.

Applying the Shingo ModelTM in an Academic Setting

Ken Snyder

The Shingo Model™ has been successfully applied in a wide range of enterprises, including manufacturing, health care, financial institutions, non-governmental organizations (NGOs), etc. The model has also been applied in a limited way within universities – mainly in administrative functions such as housing, dining services and maintenance – but, to the knowledge of the presenter, has never been applied to the core function of the university (i.e., the teaching of students). The presenter will 1) demonstrate how the Shingo Model™ can be applied in an academic setting, thereby demonstrating the universal applicability of the model; and 2) show how to revolutionize higher education by helping institutions focus on the right purpose.

Fireside Chat

Ritsuo Shingo

Former US President Franklin D. Roosevelt led his nation through two of the greatest crises in its history: the Great Depression of the 1930s and World War II. From March 1933 to June 1944 President Roosevelt addressed the American people in some 30 speeches broadcast via radio, speaking on a variety of topics from banking to unemployment to fighting fascism in Europe. Millions of people found comfort and renewed confidence in these speeches, which became known as the "fireside chats."

Today, we expand on this concept by bringing in best practitioners and leaders who have a wealth of knowledge on the pursuit of enterprise excellence and how to build a culture of continuous improvement. Spend an evening with these leaders as they share their experience with you in an intimate setting and give you valuable time to ask questions and to network with other like-minded people.

ECA's Transformational Journey

Joseph Gaysinsky

Rexam became ECA's partner in 2007. One of the company’s major contributions was the lean enterprise culture. Rexam has operated a global system of Lean Enterprise since 2003, structured around building capability in specific tool sets of Culture, VSM, 5S, TPM, SMED and Six Sigma.

Rexam’s best plants were invited to participate in a more sophisticated assessment known as “Beyond Gold,” where the implementation of lean systems is appraised. A system of merit is used to recognize achievement starting at Emerald, then Sapphire and finally Diamond. In recognition of ECA's lean efforts, the first Rexam audit was in 2012. The Guatemala plant applied for the "Beyond Gold" assessment and achieved Sapphire level, and subsequently moved to Diamond in 2013.

Improvements are identified and delivered using a matrix organization structure with functional floor leadership called “The Grill” (initiated in 2007). Between 2012 and 2014, 96 Kaizen events took place with 100 percent participation from the people of the plant.

Mr. Gaysinsky will speak to this journey while specifically focusing on “Ensuring Quality at the Source" and "Creating Value for the Customer."

Sisamex Journey of Transformation: Going from Operational Excellence to Enterprise Excellence

Manuel Valdes

Sisamex began its journey toward excellence in 1992, always striving to build a solid foundation for sustainable growth. After several years of extensive use of world-class manufacturing tools and concepts, a stop was made in 2011 to ask the questions: “What is the culture of Sisamex that has brought us here? And what should be the culture that takes us far beyond?” These questions were not easy to ask, and there were no direct answers. Sisamex had to find a business model that integrated everything they knew to do and did well, and put systems and tools to work in order to solidify a culture that guaranteed continued success well into the future. Sisamex’s strategy lacked a clearly defined "culture," until they found the Shingo Model™ that helped them translate what they were looking for. Since 2011, Sisamex has developed a culture based on measurable behaviors using systems and tools that will perfectly support its business strategy in the short, medium and long term. In this presentation, Mr. Valdes will share many of Sisamex’s experiences over the years, the good and the not so good, as well as the challenges they still face as they look forward to becoming an "ambidextrous" organization.

Equipping and Running Lean Factories with Customer-Focused Shingo Principles

Richard Schonberger

Most factories have many active products or orders seeking capacity on few producing units. If they are involved with lean, the results may be impressive WITHIN the factory. But, contrary to the Shingo Guiding Principle of Creating Value for the Customer, that narrow focus is often accompanied by extended customer lead times, backorders and inventories—with long operating hours and speed-up efforts to cope. Correctively, and in keeping with the Shingo Guiding Principle of Enterprise Alignment, concurrent production employs multiple work cells/production lines affordably equipped with simpler, lower-cost equipment. Along with customer-focused metrics, this combination allows production of multiple products/orders simultaneously, in close synch with real customer demand/usage.

Keep It Simple, Is That Possible?

Gabriel Guerra

PyMPSA, with more than 25 years of experience manufacturing medical devices, is the largest medical device manufacturing company in México and one of many companies that belong to PiSA Group, the biggest Mexican pharmaceutical company.

Over the years PyMPSA has created a lot of different processes in order to satisfy its customer´s needs and comply with the applicable regulations. Somehow some of its systems became complicated to operate, so when PyMPSA started to apply the Shingo Model, one of its goals was to improve the operation but at the same time make it simple.

In the beginning, PyMPSA started the reengineering of its processes with the typical top-bottom approach – management design, operators execute. There were some modest results until an apparent “small” and “simple” idea from a group of operators transformed the “daily management system” into the cornerstone of PyMPSA’s operation. Without any hesitation, this was a lesson of humility and is a true example of application of the Shingo Guiding Principles.

Exhibitors at the Shingo Latin America Summit enjoy the chance to discover new clients as well as reconnect with current customers in a highly relevant and social atmosphere. With plenty of networking opportunities throughout each day, you’re sure to develop new leads and contacts eager to begin a lasting, mutually beneficial relationship. Exhibitors also receive an opt-in email roster of attendees and are highlighted on the Shingo app and print items.

We offer several tax-deductible sponsorship opportunities that place your company name in front of more than 16,000 professionals and decision-makers across the globe. As a major contributor to the value and success of the Latin America Summit, you'll position your brand as a thought-leader committed to excellence. Conference attendees are mid- to senior-level management representing more than 650 companies worldwide.

The available levels of sponsorship are Friends ($5,000), Supporters ($10,000), and Benefactors ($15,000).
