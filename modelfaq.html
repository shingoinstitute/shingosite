Shingo Model™ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the Shingo Model?

A: Professor Edgar Schein of the MIT Sloan School of Management said, “The only thing of real importance that leaders do is to create and manage culture. If you do not manage culture, it manages you, and you may not even be aware of the extent to which this is happening.”

An organizational culture always forms, whether or not the leaders of the organization purposely shape it. Cultures that form without purposeful shaping can have significantly negative effects that reach all the way through the value streams of the organization.

The Shingo Model is not a set of rules to be applied, nor a roadmap to follow. Rather, it is a standard of perfection toward which organizations reach by shaping their organizational cultures accordingly. A full understanding of the Shingo Model gives a leader a clear vision of what the organization’s culture should be and a good idea of how to make it so.

Q: What is the history of the Shingo Model?

A: The Shingo Institute began its mission of recognition, education, and research 28 years ago. It was the result of collaboration between its namesake, Shigeo Shingo, and Utah State University.

Long before terms like TQM, JIT, and “lean” became popular in the western world, Dr. Shingo wrote about ensuring quality at the source, flowing value to the customer, working with zero inventory, and understanding the true working situation by being at the work site, or “going to gemba.” He worked extensively with Toyota executives to make these concepts part of the company, and the efficacy of each concept was proven during the company’s many years of industry domination.

Ever the evangelist of process improvement, Dr. Shingo envisioned a collaboration with an organization that would further his life’s work through research, practical and rigorous education, and recognition of the best in enterprise excellence worldwide.

In 1988, Utah State University collaborated with Dr. Shingo to establish the Shingo Prize, an award that recognizes organizations with cultures of sustainable excellence. Since then, the Shingo Prize has expanded into the Shingo Institute to realize Dr. Shingo’s vision of education and research. The Shingo Model is the result of Dr. Shingo’s work, as well as the collective experience and research of the Shingo Institute.

Q: How does the Shingo Model compare with other enterprise excellence models, i.e. EFQM?

A: This is one of the questions most frequently asked of the Shingo Institute. The short answer is that it isn’t just a framework for management, as other models often are. The Shingo focus is on organizational culture conducive with improvements coming directly from the mind of every associate to obtain measurable, world-class results.

Q: What is the main reason companies follow the Shingo Model?

A: Many organizations do not intend to ever challenge for the Shingo Prize but use the Shingo Model as the standard of excellence to which they aspire. Often, leaders find that lean tools, such as six sigma, jidoka, SMED, 5S, JIT, quality circles, etc., are not independently capable of effecting lasting change. Organizations that focus only on tools for improvement often get results but not the ability to sustain them. On the other hand, organizations that anchor their improvement initiatives to principles—understand the “why” behind the “how” and the “what”—experience sustainable results.

Q: How long, on average, does it take to achieve Shingo recognition?

A: The recommended submission time frame is no more than 12 months from the date you submit the application documents. Organizations do not have to wait a year to submit the achievement report. Once the application has been approved and accepted, the achievement report can arrive any time within the following 12 months.

The reason the Shingo Institute recommends a one-year time frame to submit the achievement report after the application process is to ensure there are no eligibility issues and that all questions and concerns are addressed early on in the process. The Shingo Institute also wants to make sure that time has been taken by each applicant to attend (or hold internally) a DISCOVER EXCELLENCE workshop.

Q: How many companies around the world have Shingo recognition?

A: This list grows each year. There is a list of Shingo recognition recipients at shingo.org/awards and also a historical data link which leads to the entire list going all the way back to 1989.

Q: How many companies around the world are now working towards Shingo recognition?

A: On average, there are about 10 organizations challenging for the Shingo Prize at any given time.

Q: What sort of results can companies expect when following the Shingo Model?

A: Organizations that shape their cultures to the Shingo Model create a workplace where associates feel safe enough and motivated enough to press their ideas forward to improve processes, products, and value. The result is a steady stream of ideas for continuous improvement. You can watch a testimonial video at shingo.org/model.

Q: Can SMEs work with the Shingo Model?

A: We’ve had organizations as small as 100 employees challenge for the Shingo Prize. However, any organization size can benefit by shaping its culture to the Shingo Model.

Q: What’s the best way to find out more about the Shingo Model?

A: Begin by reading the free Shingo Model handbook. It’s downloadable at shingo.org/model or your Shingo Affiliate may have hard copies you can have. To begin to understand it well enough to start shaping your organizational culture, take the DISCOVER EXCELLENCE workshop. After that, there are three other workshops that will deepen your understanding of desirable behaviors to watch for and how to elicit them. They are 1) ENABLE, 2) IMPROVE and 3) ALIGN; see shingo.org/education.html for more information.

Q: Once companies achieve Shingo recognition how do they maintain the improvements?

A: In order to keep leaders and managers on the right track, such organizations send those people to conferences and workshops. Attending these events periodically helps to refresh their memories of the Model and to reinvigorate their enthusiasm for it.
